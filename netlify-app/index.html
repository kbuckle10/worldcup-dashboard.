Learn the Basics

A plain-language guide to how the World Cup works. No prior football knowledge needed.

⚽ How scoring & match results work ›

A football match is 90 minutes (plus a little "stoppage time"). Whoever scores more goals wins. A score like 2–1 means the first team scored 2, the second scored 1.

In the group stage a match can end in a draw (tie), e.g. 1–1. In the knockout stage there must be a winner (see below).

🏆 Points: how groups are decided ›

In the group stage each team plays the other 3 teams in its group once. Results earn points:

• Win = 3 points  •  Draw = 1 point  •  Loss = 0 points

Add up the points after all 3 games. More points = higher in the table. The top teams advance.

⚖️ Tiebreakers: when teams are level on points ›

If two teams finish on the same points, FIFA separates them in this order:

1. Goal difference (goals scored minus goals conceded across all 3 games — abbreviated GD).
2. Goals scored (more is better).
3. Head-to-head — the result of the match between the tied teams.
4. Then fair-play (fewest cards) and, finally, a drawing of lots.

This dashboard sorts by points → goal difference → goals scored → head-to-head.

🎟️ The 2026 format: 48 teams & the "best 3rd place" rule ›

2026 is the first 48-team World Cup. Teams are split into 12 groups of 4 (A–L).

The top 2 of every group advance — that's 24 teams. To make a clean 32-team knockout round, the 8 best 3rd-placed teams (compared across all 12 groups by points, then goal difference, then goals) also go through.

So 32 teams reach the Round of 32, and it becomes single-elimination from there.

🥅 Knockout rounds: extra time & penalties ›

From the Round of 32 onward, a draw isn't allowed — someone must advance. If scores are level after 90 minutes:

1. Extra time — two extra 15-minute halves (30 min total). Shown as aet ("after extra time").
2. Penalty shootout — if still tied, each team takes turns shooting from the penalty spot. Shown as pens, with the shootout score in brackets, e.g. 1–1 (4–3 pens).

The rounds, in order: Round of 32 → Round of 16 → Quarter-finals → Semi-finals → Final (the two losing semi-finalists play a "third-place" match).

📖 Quick glossary ›

GF / GA — Goals For / Goals Against (scored / conceded).
GD — Goal Difference (GF minus GA).
Pld / W / D / L — Played, Won, Drawn, Lost.
Pts — Points.
Form — recent results, newest last: WDL
Clean sheet — a game where a team conceded zero goals.
aet — after extra time.  pens — decided by penalty shootout.  (OG) — own goal.
